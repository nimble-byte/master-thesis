This chapter establishes the meta-requirements that constrain the design principle space developed in Chapter~\ref{sec:design-principles}, corresponding to step~\rom{4} of the method for design principle development \parencite{Moeller2020}. It is conducted as a hermeneutic literature review \parencite{Boell2014}, organised around three thematic directions central to the research questions: the challenges of human-\ac{AI} interaction specific to \ac{GenAI} systems (Section~\ref{ssec:hai-genai}), the attribution of \ac{ToM} to \ac{AI} systems (Section~\ref{ssec:tom-attributions}), and explanations and mental model formation (Section~\ref{ssec:explanations-mental-model-formation}). The meta-requirements derived from these sections are synthesised in Section~\ref{ssec:meta-requirements}.

The starting corpus was drawn from the works introduced and discussed in Section~\ref{ssec:related-works}, which established their relevance to the three thematic directions as part of the theoretical foundation of this thesis \parencite{Boell2014}. These works were grouped into their respective directions as follows: \textcite{Weisz2025} and \textcite{Schneider2024} anchor the \ac{GenAI} interaction direction; \textcite{Jussupow2021}, \textcite{Colombatto2025}, and \textcite{Ying2024} form the basis of the \ac{ToM} attribution direction; and \textcite{Miller2019}, \textcite{Shin2021}, and \textcite{Hoffman2023} anchor the explanations and mental model direction. Foundational works such as \textcite{Arrieta2020} and \textcite{Lipton2018} were consulted as theoretical reference points but excluded as expansion anchors, as their focus on discriminative models renders much of their content superseded by the paradigm shift to \ac{GenAI} that is central to this thesis \parencite{Schneider2024}; where \ac{GenAI}-specific treatments of the same concepts exist, such as \textcite{Hoffman2023}, these are preferred.

The corpus was expanded through reverse citation search, tracing the references of the starting corpus iteratively \parencite{Boell2014}. Saturation was considered reached when new sources were found to either duplicate positions already represented in the corpus, address a scope narrower than the thematic directions, or concern implementation details rather than conceptual or empirical contributions relevant to the meta-requirements. The review is bounded thematically to the three directions identified above. The temporal boundary is set to 2017 onward, marking the introduction of the transformer architecture \parencite{Vaswani2017} as the technical inflection point that enabled modern \ac{GenAI} systems \parencite{Schneider2024}. Seminal works predating this boundary are included where they provide theoretical foundations that have not been superseded.
