\subsection{Research Paradigm} \label{ssec:research-paradigm}

\ac{DSR} is a research methodology that focuses on applying design as research method. On an abstract level, \ac{DSR} aims to generate new (design) knowledge through the creation of artefacts \parencite{Hevner2004,Vaishnavi2015}. Practically, the produced artefacts are often computing systems, including hard- or software \parencite[e.g.,][]{Toreini2018}. Theories, models, and design principles are often generated as complementary outcomes of \ac{DSR}, but can also be the primary outcome of a \ac{DSR} project \parencite[e.g.,][]{Bitzer2016, Scheider2023}.

Depending on the research setting, goals and questions researchers can choose from a variety of \ac{DSR} methodologies, which differ in their process and activities. A key distinction between them is the underlying research paradigm, which distinguish \textbf{positivist} and \textbf{interpretive} research methods \parencite{Venable2017}. Positivist methods are, based on the assumption, that there is an objective reality, that can be observed and measured. In contrast, interpretive methods are based on the assumption that reality is socially constructed and subjective, and that understanding it requires interpreting the meanings and experiences of individuals \parencite{Chen2004}. Consequently, \textcite{Venable2017} suggest selecting a positivist method when three conditions for a research project are met:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item The research topic is governed by a general set of rules or principles.
    \item An optimal solution to the problem can be found.
    \item The researcher believes that findings from research are objective.
\end{enumerate}

If any of these conditions are not met, an interpretive method should be chosen. For this thesis, the second belief is not met, as the open-ended nature of the research questions does not allow for an optimal solution. Additionally, the field of \ac{HCI} often applies qualitative methods, due to the complexity of interactions between humans and computers \parencite{Adams2008}. Therefore, this thesis will follow an interpretive research paradigm and apply an interpretive \ac{DSR} method.
