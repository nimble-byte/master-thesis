# Writing Notes

- two key research lenses for the intersection of ToM and XAI (*Wang et al. (2024a)*)
    1. equipping AI systems with ToM capabilities to improve outputs/responses and explanations (*technical/factual perspective*)
    2. attribution/perception of ToM to AI systems (*psychological/perception perspective*)
- technical perspective
    - AI does not necessarily acquire actual ToM, but rather learns to emulate ToM capabilities via shortcuts (*Aru et al. (2023)*)
        - refer to deep learning methods, that require optimising scores for a given task
        - agent might only learn to optimise reward instead of actually learning the task (e.g. identify objects based on background instead of learning object features in object recognition)
        - even if research finds ToM in AI models, it might just be emulated through shortcuts
        - interesting aspect, but of limited consequence for this thesis, since only the perception of ToM is investigated
        - does not mean that research is pointless: humans also apply shortcuts & heuristics for ToM; **Shortcuts are good**
    - ToM is more than a task, that AI can learn/solve; more of a general capability, that needs to be learned in an open ended manner (*Aru et al. (2023)*)
        - human development of ToM is likely assisted/guided by some biases (*Aru et al. (2023)*)
            - some are psychological and present basically since birth
            - some might be structural, but cognitive science has no answers to this question
        - collaborative work between agents in an open ended (i.e. not directed at one specific task) might help (*Team et al. (2021)*)
- psychological perspective
    - *Miller (2019)* notes, that ToM is required for the social process of explaining (between humans)
        - minimum is to keep track of what has been explained
        - considering knowledge & BDI to tailor explanations to user (*Halpern & Pearl (2005)*)
    - BDI for users of models plays relevant roles in the interaction (*Kashima (1998)*)
