# Writing Notes

- two key research lenses for the intersection of ToM and XAI (*Wang et al. (2024a)*):
  1. equipping AI systems with ToM capabilities to improve outputs/responses and explanations (*technical/factual perspective*)
  2. attribution/perception of ToM to AI systems (*psychological/perception perspective*)
  - note the distinction between *actual* ToM and *ascribed* ToM, but keep the subsection focused on perception regardless of whether the system truly has ToM
- technical perspective
  - AI may only emulate ToM-like behavior through shortcuts rather than acquire genuine ToM (*Aru et al. (2023)*)
  - ToM is better treated as an open-ended capability than as a single task; collaborative/open-ended settings may help explore it (*Aru et al. (2023); Team et al. (2021)*)
  - this is useful background, but not the main focus of the thesis
- psychological perspective
  - *Miller (2019)* notes that ToM is part of the social process of explaining
  - tailoring explanations requires considering user knowledge and BDI (*Halpern & Pearl (2005); Kashima (1998)*)
