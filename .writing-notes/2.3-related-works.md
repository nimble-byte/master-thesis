# Writing Notes

## Narrative Structure for Related Works in Fundamentals

This section serves as a *conceptual bridge* that categorizes the landscape of related research without deep synthesis. It identifies two complementary research directions that interact:

1. **Technical/Engineering direction**: Equipping AI systems with ToM-like capabilities or probing models for belief-like representations.
   - Examples: model-level belief reasoning, chain-of-thought techniques, evaluating if models develop internal models of users.
   - Brief mention only; technical details and empirical findings move to Problem Awareness chapter.

2. **Psychological/Perceptual direction**: How people attribute mental states, beliefs, and intentions to artificial agents.
   - Examples: anthropomorphism studies, BDI frameworks for understanding user mental models, how users interpret explanations.
   - Anchored to the cognitive frameworks already introduced (dual-process, heuristics, false-belief concepts).
   - Again, brief framing here; deep literature synthesis in Problem Awareness.

## Positioning in the thesis

- **Fundamentals (brief)**: One concise subsection (1–2 paragraphs) that names the two research directions, notes they are distinct but interacting, and sets up the forward reference to Chapter 4 (Problem Awareness).
- **Problem Awareness (deep)**: Literature synthesis subsections that:
  - Systematically review empirical findings in each direction
  - Identify conflicting results and gaps
  - Synthesize how both directions inform the design of explainable generative AI for users
  - Derive meta-requirements from the literature gaps

## Key point to convey

The related works subsection in Fundamentals clarifies that the thesis spans two research questions, not one. It shows that technical advances in AI ToM are only half the story if users don't perceive or trust those advances; conversely, understanding user perception is only actionable if we know what technical capabilities exist to support better explanations.
