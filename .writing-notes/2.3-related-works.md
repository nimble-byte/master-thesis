# Writing Notes

- **Design principles for generative AI (Weisz et al. 2025, Handbook of Human-Centered AI, Springer)**: Six practitioner-validated design principles for generative AI UX; addresses the shift from command-based to specification-based interaction — directly relevant to designing explanations for generative AI
- **Mental models and AI task performance (Jussupow et al., 2021, Information Systems Research)**: Demonstrates that the accuracy of a user's mental model of AI is a direct determinant of whether AI assistance improves or degrades decision quality; motivates the design question of how explanations can support accurate model formation
- **Trust calibration through AI transparency (Ying & Gajos, 2024, ToMinHAI@CHI)**: Empirical evidence that explicitly communicating an AI's belief state about user goals improves trust calibration in collaborative tasks; bridges machine-ToM output and attribution effects
- **MToM framework (Wang & Goel, 2024, ToMinHAI@CHI)**: Proposes a three-stage process (construction → recognition → revision) and three content elements (interpretation, feedback, mutuality); provides the most direct theoretical framing for how user-facing AI behaviour shapes ToM attribution
- **Mental-state attribution and trust in LLMs (Colombatto et al., 2025, Communications Psychology)**: Pre-registered experiment showing attribution of consciousness and mentality to LLMs predicts reliance behaviour — establishes the empirical link between ToM attribution and user behaviour for generative AI specifically
